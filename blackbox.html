Mystery Black Box Investigation

Introduction:

In our ninth grade New Media and Technology class we have this box called the mystery black box where water goes in and sometimes the same or different amounts of water come back out. It is our job to figure out what is inside of the box. We would measure the amount of water that is poured in and the amount we receive after it leaves the box. This project we do is ESUMS specific because our teacher (Mr. Sinuses) made the mystery black box and only he knows what is inside of it.

Claim:

I believe that inside of the mystery black box is a box right under the funnel and off of it are 3 tubes that are different speeds connected 1 tube for it to come out at the end. The water gets distributed between the tubes and some of them have sponges which absorb the water and slow it down.

Evidence:

How we tested Mr. Sinuses black box was by pouring in 1000ml of water each time into the black box and recording our data in the data table of how much we got out and what we observed. For example the first 2 trials we poured in 1000ml and got 0ml of water back out. This shows that whatever is inside can hold at least 2000ml at a time. There were a total of 48 trials done with this mystery black box and they were all recorded and put into the data table. It is hard to figure out exactly how much is in the box at a time because some of the trials got messed up by water spilling from mistakes.

Homemade Spreadsheet

To build our model we took a cardboard box and made a hole in the top and bottom for the funnels. We cut holes in the buckets and put tubes in so they were attached, we used lots of tape to secure things to the box. The method my group did to test our model was by pouring the same amount (100ml) of water each time into the box. We kept pouring 100ml into the box until we got a result of water coming out. Our approach worked pretty well because we inserted all of our data into a data table clearly. At this point in the process we discovered some leaks and cracks in our model that we did not know were there but it ended up helping us because we were able to discover more about our handmade model. We did a total of 4 trials each pouring in 100ml and after the first 2 trials of getting nothing out we had the second one get 100ml out and the third one getting 47ml out. We used certain materials to make out mystery black box here are the following.

  • Cardboard
  • Three plastic tubes
  • Sponges
  • Exacto knife
  • Two funnels
  • Bucket with a whole at the bottom
  • Tape
  • Water
  • Hot glue
  • Hot glue gun
  • Drill

Reasoning:

Although we had a lot of leaks I still believe this could be in the mystery black box. If we made it a little stronger we would have got a different result. Given that even with the leaks we got water out goes to show that we would have been able to get more clearer results without the leaks. Models are useful because it shows your outlook and your thinking behind it in a physical copy instead of leaving it in your head.

Photography with Professional dSLR cameras:

The photography part of this project was essentially to capture the process it took including the building, designing and testing. We used different angles by standing on a chair to make the subject look small or getting on the floor and making the subject big and overpowering. We used the rule of thirds by moving to make the subject in the thirds instead of centered every time because then it gets repetitive and boring. We were able to use this as a way to show off our model and our experiences and ways of building it, testing it and designing it in pictionary.

2D design and animation in adobe photoshop

My initial idea was that there was a tipping bucket. I then shortly realized It would be hard for me to create that and it does not change the amount that comes out each time. As a result of this I came up with my new idea of the three different tubes that are different speeds to slow down the water that comes out. In photoshop we used tablets connected to our computers so our brush work was more visible and we could add fine details so we could get our ideas across as clearly as possible. Another thing we did in photoshop was creating frame by frame animations. We did this by selecting which frames were visible by layers in our design. By doing this it was easier to see how our designs worked before we built them.

3D design and animation in Autodesk Maya

We used this platform called Autodesk Maya to create our blackboxes on an online platform. We also mixed in some animation to visualize how it worked and how it looked from different angles (camera views). I started out just by using basic tools and by the end I was able to use the tools like bevel, sweep mesh and boolean. I used the basic settings like rotate, scale, size and move first and then by the end of it I was able to add curves for my tubes and make them translucent so I could see the water going through. I used the boolean tool in order to mold some of my shapes together and I extruded them to give them some depth. Another thing we did was experiencing the smooth and rough edges to give a softer or sharper look. I used the bevel tool in order to create a hole in the top of my box to have a hole for the funnel to go through. I was able to sign everything with a specific material and color, so it looked nicer than them all being the same gray. I learned how to rig my hand model to get it to wave and move back and forth. I animated the door to my box, the graduated cylinder with pouring water, an arrow to show the water and my camera. After we finished our models we rendered them which took lots of time and we got our models looking nice in the animations. All of these things went together nicely for a complete mystery blackbox animation.
