Sasha Capelle's Screenprinting Web Page

What was this assignment about?

The project we did in our 9th grade New Media and Tech class was creating an online version of our screen printed models that we designed in photoshop. We did this by first deciding what mattered to us and a way we wanted to represent that certain idea. I chose thinking in different perspectives because it is something that matters to me and I think is valuable and important. I was interested in explaining how when you see things through different perspectives it can reflect on your outlook on things. When researching I found a lot of images that show multiple perspectives and they reflected new different ways of thinking

Procedure for the screenprint project

1. We started by researching ideas that we would like our message to be about.
2. Began to map out and draw our ideas in photoshop.
3. Took pictures of ourselves to incorporate in our designs and editted them to match what we were looking for.
4. Began drawing on the photos for the design in colors of our choice.
5. Next we did a dimension guide to map out the wood frames we had to make for the prints, we did this step in photoshop making it easier when we cut the wood.
6. We chose the wood for our frames and then marked dimensions with a pencil to the certain lengths.
7. We cut all the wood pieces for the frame. (In my case, 8 pieces cut for 2 frames).
8. Next we glued the frame together with wood glue and applied staples to hold it in place.
9. After our base frame was complete we stretched mesh screen over our frame and stapled it to the frame. We put tape over the staples so no loose staples could tear anyones screens.
10 Now that the frame is completed we cut out or vinyl designs.
11. After the vinyl was cut out we weeded the vinyl to what we wanted exposed to paint.
12. We then heat pressed the vinyl onto our frames by using irons with protective layers in between.
13. Now you are ready to print!
14. You print by locking in your frames with brackets and scooping paint on.
15. You squiggy the paint onto the frame and slide the paint across revealing a cool design on what is being printed on!

All about my design!

For my design I chose to represent looking in different perspectives. I chose this because I think it is personal and important to me because I've always thought that it was benefical if you look at things in a new light instead of the same way every time. I chose to pick a design where there us two main ways to look at it, a tree or a brain. My resoning for this was that when I asked people some people said tree right away and others said brain. I think it is cool how you can see different things just based one who you are and what perspective you have. This is a really important thing to learn and it is good that people can talk about it, lets say someone who sees a brain and someone who sees a tree talk about it, then now their perspectives have changed and they start to think in different ways and experiencing different outlooks on the way we think. I printed this design on a box that later became a review card, leitner box and I printed it on paper and fabric.
