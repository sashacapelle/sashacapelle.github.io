Genetics- In New Media and Tech

What was the project we did in class?

In class we did a project where we extracted DNA from our cheek cells in our mouths. We first made predictions of who in our grade we thought we would match/ have the same DNA as. Majority of the grade said they would match with people of the same race as them because it is easy to assume DNA has a direct relation to race, but it turns out that is completely wrong. Let me tell you how I know...

Claim

Race is not a biologically real concept even though it is easy to assume it is.

Evidence

In our new media and technology for social change class in ninth grade we did an experiment of extracting DNA to find who had similar/ the same DNA as us.

We did this by swabbing some cheek cells from our mouth and swirling them into the extraction buffer to dislodge the cells. The next step was to incubate the tube with our DNA and buffer in it to heat up the cells so they can be bursted open. After the DNA spent enough time heating up we pipetted the DNA into the wells of agarose gel. Since DNA is negatively charged we used magnets that pulled the DNA towards the positive electrode through the gel leaving us with data of the distance our DNA traveled.

We took all of this data into account and organized it into a table to find out who we matched meaning who we had the same amount of copies as. The majority of the grade predicted they would match someone that was a similar race or hair texture as them. This was partially correct for example I predicted I would get someone from a different class that was also mixed with black and white and we did match but what came as a surprise to me was that I also matched was someone that was middle eastern/north african and someone who was hispanic/latino. For the most part the DNA matching is unpredictable which shows how race is not biologically real as we can see from this DNA lab.

Reasoning

To say that race is biologically real would mean that just because you are the same race as someone means that you are automatically related and would have similar genetics or more specifically the same DNA frequencies as them. We know this can be proven incorrect because in our DNA lab from class we did not match with only people of our same race. Most people matched with people of completely different races who they would never even have guessed.

To say that race is not a biologically real concept would mean that even if you have the same race as someone does not mean you would directly match your DNA with them or other aspects within their bodies.

If biological race was real then when we look at maps of skin tone and blood type they should be the same however this is not the case. When looking at a map of skin color and blood types across the world it is not proven to be the same for each skin color to match the same blood type, it appears to be random. For example in the map Australia is all Medium dark skin tone but the blood types are varied from 0-5% Type B and 1-20%, 50% Type A. If biological race was real then all the people with the same skin tone would have the same blood type but we know that is not true proven with the skin tone and blood type map explained above.
